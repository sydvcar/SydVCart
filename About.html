Meet Sydney Carmer

My name is Sydney Carmer (she/they/he), also known by my online handle SydVC, and I am a multimedia artist from in North Carolina. Currently, I am based in California, pursuing my MFA in Cinematography at UCLA. I graduated from The University of North Carolina at Charlotte in May 2024 with a BFA in Art, double concentrating in Digital Media and Illustration. I was also in the Arts + Architecture Honors Program, and double minored in Psychology and Art History. I specialize in filmmaking, post-production/video editing, and 2D animation/motion graphics, but my visual arts background makes me experienced in a wide range of media. I'm an organized, quick learner who is always ready to adapt to feedback. My work can be playful, childlike, and funny, but also spooky, dark, and emotional. The intersection of these contrasting aesthetics is where I thrive, encouraging viewers to engage with strange and taboo topics. From behind a camera or computer screen to inside an art gallery, my passion for community and experimentation fuels my creative research. Outside of art, I spend my time watching movies, playing soccer, reading, and playing card games with my family. Please don't hesitate to reach out through my business email: sydvcart@gmail.com

Undergraduate Honors Thesis

My Honors Thesis for the Arts + Architecture Honors Program at UNC Charlotte was entitled Histories of New Media: The Contemporary Discourse of Digital Arts. As part of the research project, I created a syllabus for a new media art history seminar and studio hybrid course at the undergraduate university level. This included five units complete with readings, key artists/artwork, and accompanying studio assignments. The emerging field of "new media" or "digital arts" has become a pressing topic for artists and art historians alike in recent years, but this discourse-the statements surrounding digital art practice and the artifacts that result-is anything but novel. I chose to explore the intimate relationship between art and science by analyzing the history of new media and revealing an ongoing transformation of its related discourse rather than reducing it to a simple rivalry or novelty. This research was also presented and awarded at the 2024 Undergraduate Research Conference at UNC Charlotte. Read my full thesis here.
